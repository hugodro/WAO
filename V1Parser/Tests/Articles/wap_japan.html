Revolutionary Switch To 3G Needed: Analyst

November 8, 1999 -- The switch to broader-band third-generation (3G) wireless technology must be revolutionary, not evolutionary, and much of that revolution already is coming from Japan, according to one wireless industry analyst.

"If the 3G revolution is to provide value for end-users, it must come from new, expanded or improved applications," said Dr. Herschel Shosteck, president of Herschel Shosteck Associates. "Raw bandwidth by itself won't do."

He is an author of a new study entitled Third Generation Wireless (3G): Why, When and How It Will Happen. He claims that some of the big winners in this so-called 3G revolution may be currently small companies.

The study also discusses why some of the big winners in 3G technology may not be companies that currently are well known.

"Outsiders are not constrained by the vested economic and technology interests of those within the industry," said Jane Zweig, executive vice president of the firm. "Nor are they constrained by the present viewpoints of the industry. As such, outsiders can develop a vision of the future that extends beyond the comprehension of those presently in the wireless world."

The study also projects that Japanese firms will dominate the market for 3G-compliant devices because they hold as much as a two year lead in R&D over their European and North American competitors.
