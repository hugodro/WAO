Nokia In WAP Pact With Telia

November 25, 1999 (Reuters) -- Finnish telecoms equipment group Nokia said on Thursday it would supply its Artus Messaging Platform to Swedish Telia's mobile phone operator for an undisclosed sum.

Nokia said in a statement that the order would allow Telia, which recently merged with Norwegian Telenor, to extend its current array of Wireless Application Protocol (WAP) services.

WAP is an increasingly-used method of delivering data to small-screen wireless devices such as smart phone.  Nokia is one of the largest manufacturers of such devices and it also supplies WAP infrastructure products for companies such as Telia.
