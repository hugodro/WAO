Vendors Predict Strong WAP Phone Demand

November 30, 1999 (Reuters) -- Two major vendors of wireless Internet technology say the signs indicate that Wireless Application Protocol (WAP)-based connections are about to become extremely popular.

Top executives for Finnish mobile phone maker Nokia say the company is experiencing unprecedented demand for its first WAP smart phones.  Nokia started shipments of its first WAP phone, called the 7110 or the Nokia Mediaphone, at the end of September.

Nokia's Chairman and Chief Executive Jorma Ollila said Nokia had already delivered around 100,000 units of the model and that the company was ramping up production to meet demand.

Matti Alahuhta, the head of the group's mobile phones unit Nokia Mobile Phones, said demand for the 7110 model was much stronger than Nokia had expected. "I cannot immediately think of anything similar," Alahuhta told reporters on Monday, referring to what Nokia has seen for its other models to date.

In addition, a senior executive for Phone.com, which provides WAP technology to phone vendors such as Nokia, predicted that more than half the world's predicted one billion mobile phone subscribers in 2003 will be connected to the Internet.

"The millions (of WAP phone unit sales) will come in the first and second quarter of next year," said Malcolm Bird, the company's Managing Director and Vice President, Europe.

Building on a "thin client" model, where users need only lightweight equipment to handle information on a remote, networked system, Phone.com executives say the company's prime goal is to generate sales of its server equipment. It has stimulated the market through the royalty-free distribution of a microbrowser -- the software engine for a cell phone to navigate the Net -- and it is distributing a wireless portal or Web gateway for operators to brand in their own names.

"The browser is a profitable business but not the main revenue-generator," Bird said.
