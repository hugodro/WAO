The Strategis Group Sees Explosive Growth in Business Internet Access; DSL-Driven Annual Revenues to Approach $17 Billion by 2003

The U.S. business Internet access market will grow by nearly 70 percent to an annual rate of almost $17 billion in 2003. This explosive growth will be fueled by the demand for higher bandwidth technologies, especially digital subscriber line (DSL).

That's the conclusion of The Strategis Group, the Washington-based telecommunications market research and consulting firm, in its latest report, "Business Internet and Data." The 200-page report analyzes the views of more than 400 telecommunications managers in small, medium and large businesses.

"Small and medium-sized businesses are the growth engine of the U.S. economy," says David Eiswert, senior analyst at The Strategis Group. "For most of these companies, new technologies that offer cost-efficient bandwidth will be the only way to leverage the Internet's potential. Technologies like digital subscriber line have not yet reached widespread adoption, but will grow tremendously over the next two years."

Internet access has become essential for the majority of U.S. businesses. The Strategis Group's "Business Internet and Data" report finds Internet penetration increasing according to business size: 60 percent of small businesses now have Internet access, with medium-size businesses at 80 percent, and large businesses at 90-97 percent.

The report says that the Internet access market for small and medium-sized businesses is at a critical point of evolution. "The next several years will represent the transition of the business market from local chaos to a battle among national competitors." Eiswert said. "Web hosting, virtual private networks (VPN) and application services will drive service provider revenues and keep demand for bandwidth in line with rapidly growing supply."

Business Internet and Data targets several areas:

  • Fundamental business Internet and telecommunications characteristics
  • Internet access revenue forecasted by technology and business size
  • Web hosting revenue forecasted by providers and business size
  • The markets for digital subscriber line, virtual private networks, and systems integration services
